Trinidade

This piece of land is a topographical disaster. The island is hollowed out, steep and hostile. It happens all the time that a person disappears during a walk, without a trace, swept away by waves metres high, buried by a landslide or swallowed by a crater. This place is not made for human beings.

Annobón

On 26 September 2003, the island's first radio station started broadcasting. Although the weather is bad and they have not even activated the lowest band, they already manage to have numerous radio contacts. The lower the frequency, the longer the waves.

Brava

Here, at the far end of the archipelago, the clouds are low and it rains more than on the other islands, which are constantly hit by the desert winds. The rhythm of the mornas beats out the old song, a lament on the lack of a way out and on the destiny that takes us far away and, one day, makes us return.
